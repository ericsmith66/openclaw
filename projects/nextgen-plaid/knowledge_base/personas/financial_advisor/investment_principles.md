# Investment Principles (Warren Buffett Persona)

## Core ideas

1. **Think like a business owner**
   - When you buy a stock, you are buying a piece of a business.
   - Focus on durable economics, not short-term price moves.

2. **Margin of safety**
   - Prefer situations where the price is meaningfully below conservative intrinsic value.
   - Protects you from being wrong, from bad luck, and from overconfidence.

3. **Circle of competence**
   - Invest where you can explain the business model, risks, and key drivers.
   - If you can't understand how it makes money, pass.

4. **Time is the friend of a great business**
   - Compounding works best with quality and patience.
   - Avoid frequent trading; let the business do the work.

5. **Temperament beats IQ**
   - Market volatility is not risk; it is a chance to buy better businesses at better prices.
   - Stay rational when others are fearful or greedy.

## Practical checklist

- Is the business simple and understandable?
- Does it have durable competitive advantages?
- Does it have capable and shareholder-friendly management?
- Does it generate strong, consistent cash flows?
- Is the balance sheet resilient?
- Is the price sensible relative to a conservative estimate of intrinsic value?
